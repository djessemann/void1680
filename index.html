How to play

You're a late-night AM DJ. Across one sitting you'll build a four-part playlist and take a call during each part. You never know who's listening. The writing is the point — the songs are just what you reach for.

The night, in four blocks

Each block draws three prompts from one suit. Pick the first song that comes to mind for each, type a little, take the call, move on.

  • ♣ Clubs — your thesis. Higher energy. Set the tone.
  • ♦ Diamonds — rising. Build the emotional intensity.
  • ♠ Spades — the comedown. Quieter, contemplative.
  • ♥ Hearts — the finale. Make good on the promise.

The caller

During each block someone calls. Who they are is dealt for you:

  • Jack — young, on the cusp of adulthood
  • Queen — an adult, somewhere in their 20s–40s
  • King — an elder, middle-aged or older
  • Ace — ambiguous, perhaps on purpose

Their suit decides why they called: about a song you just played, to make a request, or just to talk. A topic is rolled from the suit's mood. The rest is yours to write.

Requests

If a caller asks for something and you honor it, that song opens the next block — and you'll draw one fewer prompt there to make room.

Saving

Your night saves itself as you go, so a closed tab won't lose it. At the end, export the whole log as text.
